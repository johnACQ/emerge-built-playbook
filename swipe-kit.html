The negative-keyword wall

We block the wrong searches from day one: "jobs," "rent," "for sale," "DIY," "free," "shipping container house," competitor brand terms, and the wrong province's language, so you never pay for a click that can't become a customer.

C

Objections, angles & the damaging admission

The 4 objections, answered

  • "It's too expensive." Break it against 15+ years of rent, the equity added, and the $80K loan at 2%. A suite is an asset, not a cost.
  • "Is it even legal on my lot?" That's the whole hook. The qualifier answers it in 3 minutes with their real address.
  • "Modular feels cheap / temporary." CSA-approved, 40+ patents, steel and concrete, 4 to 26 storeys. This is heavier-built than a site frame, not lighter.
  • "How long will it take?" Factory build runs while the site is prepped, then one crane day. Weeks, not the years a custom build takes.

The damaging admission to lead with

Trust is won by saying the true hard thing first. Ours: "Modular isn't right for everyone. If you want a fully custom, hand-built home with a hundred changes along the way, we're not your builder. What we do is deliver a proven, factory-precise home, fast, at a price we can actually quote." It filters the wrong buyer and makes the right one lean in.

Same energy as your own line: no one gives a damn how good the factory is, they want to know what it does for them.
