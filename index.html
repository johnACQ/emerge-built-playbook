The opportunity

The wind is at your back harder than it has ever been. Three things are happening at once: a housing shortage nobody can hand-build their way out of, a wave of law changes that made a second home legal on nearly every lot out west, and cheap government money to pay for it. Factory-built is the only answer that scales. That's you.

The math doesn't work without you

Canada has to roughly double its home-building pace, and CMHC says outright it can't be done "without a significantly larger and modernized workforce."1 But nearly one in four tradespeople in Alberta and BC leaves the site for good within about seven years.3 Nobody can hammer their way out of this. The only lever that bends the curve is moving the labour indoors, into a factory. That is exactly what your 40-plus patents and your one-floor-of-units-a-day crane process already do. The story writes itself: where local trades are scarce, or don't exist at all like you said about Prince George, you show up with a finished home and a crew for a week.

The law just handed you a market

BC's Bill 44 killed single-family-only zoning province-wide. Three to four units are now legal as-of-right on essentially every residential lot, up to six near transit.5 Edmonton allows up to eight units on most low-density lots.6 Overnight, hundreds of thousands of ordinary backyards became legal build sites. Most of the homeowners who own them still have no idea. That gap between "it's legal now" and "nobody told me" is the entire opportunity, and it has a clock on it: Calgary's blanket rezoning is set to be repealed in Aug 2026, with earlier applications grandfathered.7

02

Why residential, and why now

You were clear you don't want the whole factory turned into laneway homes, and you shouldn't. This fills your ~40,000 sq ft residential line and keeps it full, no more, no less. Residential is where we start for three reasons.

Reachable

This buyer is findable

Unlike your developer clients, the homeowner building a backyard suite Googles "cost to build a laneway house" and scrolls Instagram at night. Vancouver alone has built 7,000-plus laneway homes since 2009.10 You can find this person with paid media. You cannot find a land baron that way.

Urgent

The clock is real

Calgary's rezoning repeal in Aug 2026 and its suite incentive going to a waitlist in June 20268 are genuine "build now or miss it" levers. We point ads at real deadlines, never invented ones. Damaging-admission honesty is what builds trust with this buyer.

Uncontested

The field is asleep

The modular and ADU builders out west hide their pricing, offer no financing help, and never tell the rental-income story. The two brands that do the opposite are the two that win.11 We copy what works and out-execute everyone coasting.

03

The buyer

We're not selling to "homeowners." We're selling to four specific people, each with a different reason to build and each needing a different ad. Getting this right is 80% of why the campaign works. Every one of these is a residential buyer, exactly the person you described.

1. The caregiver

"I want Mom close, but not under the same roof." Your words on the call. Multigenerational households are the fastest-growing household type in the country, up 21% since 2001.9 Emotional, not price-shopping. We sell dignity, independence, and a suite in the backyard that beats a care home.

2. The parent of an adult kid

"My son can't afford to move out for university." Roughly 2.5 million Canadians over 25 still live with their parents.9 This buyer wants their kid launched and their home worth more. We sell a place for them to live now that becomes a rental later.

3. The income investor

"I want the backyard to pay me." A Vancouver laneway rents for $2,000 to $3,200 a month and lifts the property value by a large share of build cost.12 We sell the payback math and the equity, with a calculator right on the page.

4. The lot owner doing a duplex or fourplex

"Bill 44 says I can put four units here." Owns a lot, sees the new zoning, has never built. We sell certainty: fixed factory price, one crane day, no trade-shortage delays, a crew in and out in a week. Your model, not a promise.

Where Alberta differs, and the objection nobody answers

Albertans don't type "laneway." They type "backyard suite," "garage suite," "garden suite." We buy every local word so we're not paying for the wrong province's language. And the real objection for all four is "how do I pay for it." Ottawa now lends up to $80,000 at 2% over 15 years to build a suite.8 Almost nobody in the market is telling homeowners that. We will, and it's the single biggest unlock in the plan.

The landscape

I pulled apart how every modular and ADU builder out west markets itself. They compete on product and neglect the funnel. Here's the field, and the seven open doors we walk through.

Who's out there

  • ROC Modular (S. Alberta), your closest peer. Markets like a contractor: no pricing, thin galleries, "Contact Us." Beatable on the funnel.
  • Honomobo (Edmonton), the best-marketed player and the bar. Shows prices openly, has a financing page, strong PR.11
  • Smallworks / Lanefab (Vancouver), own the "laneway cost" content and SEO by publishing full price breakdowns.
  • Modworks (Edmonton/Calgary), closest AB ADU peer, and asleep: no pricing, no financing, no ROI story.
  • Stack Modular, manufactures in Shanghai. Wide open to a "built in Alberta, no ocean freight, no tariff risk" attack.

The seven gaps we exploit

  • Pricing opacity, everyone hides it; the two who publish, win. We publish a "starting from" and a calculator.
  • No financing guidance, we own the $80K federal loan and refi conversation.
  • No rental-income story, we make the suite pay for itself on the page.
  • Decayed local SEO, "garage suite builder Edmonton" is under-defended.
  • "Built in Alberta" is unclaimed, local steel, local jobs, no offshore risk. You own it.
  • No configurators, a pick-a-model, get-an-estimate tool would lead the category.
  • Nobody uses the CSA angle, "factory-approved, the city doesn't re-inspect" is a trust bomb nobody's throwing.

The takeaway: the winners win on transparency, content and financing, not a better box. You already have the best box, plus a "27 years, 40-plus patents, one floor a day, CSA-approved" story none of them can match. We say it louder and put a funnel under it.

M1 · The message

The first of the three Ms every engine runs on: Message, Marketing, Method. Message is the words and the offer. You nailed the brief yourself: sell the six-pack, not the chicken and rice. Nobody buys structural steel. They buy their mother living next door.

The core reposition

Away from "faster, greener, quieter construction," which is about you, toward the outcome the buyer pictures: "A second home in your backyard. Built in our factory, craned in, livable in weeks not years." Your proof points, 27 years, 40-plus patents, CSA-approved, half the time of a site build, become the reasons-to-believe underneath the promise, never the headline.

The offer that converts

A $200K-plus decision stalls on uncertainty. We kill it with one concrete, low-friction first step: "Find out what your lot qualifies for and what your suite would cost, free, in 3 minutes." That's the lead magnet, the ad promise, and the landing-page headline, all matched. Not "request a consultation." A specific, valuable, instant answer. The full page is built out in The Funnel →

The one-page story (Rule of One)

Your backyard is now legal to build on → most people don't know → here's what yours qualifies for → here's the suite → here's what it rents for and adds to your home → here's how to pay for it → book a call. Every ad, page and email tells one slice of that story. One reader, one promise, one next step.

Phase 2 · Scale the residential engine

Once the ads are printing booked calls, we widen the residential funnel without touching the developer or land-JV side. This is where your own best ideas get built into the machine.

The GIS "productized lot" play

Your idea, and a strong one. We take one proven design, use GIS to find the 50 lots in a neighbourhood with the same geometry, and hand agents a ready product to sell: fixed factory price, only the site work varies. It turns your suite into a listing a realtor can market to a whole street, not a one-off custom job.

The territory reseller network

Also yours: local product experts by territory who sell the residential units in their town on an eat-what-you-kill basis, while you keep control of the marketing and the brand. We build the recruiting funnel and the partner kit, the same lead machine, pointed at recruiting resellers instead of buyers.

The referral flywheel

Suites cluster. The neighbour sees the crane, the finished unit, the happy owner. We wire a referral and reviews engine so every completed build feeds the next, and agents and mortgage brokers get a co-branded "finance your suite" kit that keeps sending you deals.

Deliberately out of scope

Your experienced-developer capacity and the land-JV, real-estate-curious work are not in this plan. They sell on relationships, procurement and Greg's network, not paid ads, and I'd be lying if I told you a Facebook campaign wins them. This playbook stays entirely on the residential line. When you're ready to talk about the bigger B2B pipeline, that's a separate conversation with a separate plan.
